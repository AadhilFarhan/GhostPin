Why GhostPin

macOS has no "always on top." Click into another app and the window you were watching disappears. GhostPin keeps it in sight.

📌

Pin any window

Pick any open window from the menu bar — video, call, terminal, chat. A floating mirror stays above every app, every Space, even full-screen apps.

🎞️

Live, not a snapshot

Powered by Apple's ScreenCaptureKit: a real-time stream at up to 30 fps, GPU-composited. The source can be buried or on another desktop — the mirror keeps playing.

👻

Ghost mode

Tap the eye badge on any mirror's corner (or press ⌥⌘G) and it turns semi-transparent and click-through — see it, but click and type straight through it. The badge stays clickable on a ghosted mirror, so one tap always brings it back.

↔️

Any size, anywhere

Drag it anywhere, resize from any edge with the aspect ratio locked, double-click to flip between thumbnail and large, dial in opacity from the hover controls.

⌨️

Global hotkeys

Pin the frontmost window, toggle ghost mode, or clear everything without touching the mouse — and without needing the Accessibility permission.

🔒

Private by design

Zero network code. Frames go from capture straight to your screen — nothing recorded, stored, or uploaded. All source public under MIT.

Three hotkeys, whole app

They work system-wide, whatever app you're in — and none are required: every mirror carries an eye badge that toggles click-through with one click.

⌥ ⌘ PPin / unpin the frontmost window
⌥ ⌘ GGhost mode — click-through all pins
⌥ ⌘ UUnpin everything

How it works

Apple provides no public way to force another app's window to stay on top — that's an OS restriction, not a missing feature. GhostPin doesn't fight it. Instead of moving the target window, it streams a live capture of it into a small floating panel that GhostPin fully controls: always on top, optionally transparent, optionally click-through.

The one honest trade-off: the mirror is view-only. To pause the video or reply to the chat, click the original window as usual — the mirror reflects it instantly. That trade is exactly what makes the whole thing possible without private APIs or hacks.

Install in two minutes

GhostPin isn't notarized by Apple yet, so first launch takes one extra step.

Homebrew user? brew install --cask aadhilfarhan/tap/ghostpin — then skip to step 2.

Download and drag to Applications

Get the latest .dmg, open it, and drag GhostPin into Applications.

Allow the app to open

Double-click GhostPin, dismiss the warning, then go to System Settings → Privacy & Security and click Open Anyway. One time only.

Grant Screen Recording

Approve the prompt, enable GhostPin under Screen & System Audio Recording, and reopen the app. The pin appears in your menu bar — pin your first window. This permission exists because the mirror reads window pixels; capture is video-only and nothing is ever recorded, stored, or uploaded — GhostPin has no network code.
